Global Database Tools

  • Global Database Search

    • Use a Global Database Search tool to search the whole MPS-Db from the Home page. To search:
      • Go to the MPS-Db Home page
      • Type a search term into the global search bar
      • Type a space and add an additional term(s) (optional)
      • Make preliminary selection (hit enter, click the blue Search button, or mouse down the options list to item of interest and click the left mouse button)
      • Wait for a list of matching information to appear
      • Click to check (or uncheck) filter boxes in the filter section to further restrict (or unrestrict) matching results (optional)
      • Click on provided link to be routed to desired location

  • Table Searching and Exporting

    • In many places throughout the database, there are lists/tables with search bars located at the top. To search:
      • Type a search term in the search bar
      • Type a space and add an additional term(s) (optional)
      • From the list, click on the View/Edit button or click an active link to be routed to desired location

    • Help Notes:
      • Above search bars are options to copy the table to the clipboard, download the table to CSV, or print the table
      • The Column visibility button can be used to turn columns on/off in the list/table
      • To sort the list/table, click on the arrow in the column header
      • To sort on multiple columns, click on the arrow on the primary sort column, then hold the shift key and click on additional column arrows
      • Click the blue to see hidden fields

  • Download Data Button

    • There are many locations in the database where a Download button is provided. To download:
      • Click the Download button to download data
      • If shown, check/uncheck box for changing data filtering options (optional)
      • Use options for filtering, if provided (optional)

    • Assay Data Visualization (Intra-Study)

      The Assay Data Visualization (Intra-Study) feature is accessed from within an individual study, pre-filtered as follows:
      1) each treatment group,
      2) each plate (if using a plate),
      3) each chip or well, and
      4) the whole study.

      • Assay Data Visualization - Each Treatment Group:
        • Go to the Studies tab/icon
        • Click on the View/Edit button next to the study of interest
        • Click on the View button next to the group of interest
        • Review metadata and, if displayed, the assay data point graphs

      • Assay Data Visualization - Each Plate:
        • Go to the Studies tab/icon
        • Click on the View/Edit button next to the study of interest
        • Scroll down to the Plates section and click on the View/Edit button next to the plate of interest
        • Review metadata and, if displayed, the assay data point graphs

      • Assay Data Visualization - Each Chip or Well:
        • Go to the Studies tab/icon
        • Click on the View/Edit button next to the study of interest
        • Scroll down to the Chips and Wells section and click on the View/Edit button next to the chip/well of interest
        • Review metadata and, if displayed, the assay data point graphs
        • Review assay data point table
        • Note: The assay data point table offers the data group editor and data group administrator of the study the ability to include/exclude assay data points from being included/excluded from graphs and analyses

      • Assay Data Visualization - Whole Study:
        • Go to the Studies tab/icon
        • Click on the View/Edit button next to the study of interest
        • Click the Analyze/Edit button and select the Study Summary option
        • Review metadata and, if displayed, the assay data point graphs
        • {% include 'help_customization_group_filter_links.html' %}

    • Assay Data Power Analysis

      A statistical power analysis methodology has been implemented in the MPS-Db to estimate the probability that a statistical test will find a significant difference between two different data groups, such as control and compound treatment groups (post hoc two-sample power analysis), or a significant difference from one treatment group (a priori one-sample power analysis) for an MPS model.

      • To perform power analysis:
        • Go to the Studies tab/icon
        • Click on the View/Edit button next to the study of interest
        • Click on the Analyze/Edit button
        • Select the Power Analysis option
        • Use the Custom Filtering for Data Visualization (sidebar) to change grouping or filtering of data
        • Check the box next to the set of interest to see the results of interest
        • Select one or two sample power analysis
        • Scroll down and select two compound groups for two sample or one compound group for one sample
        • Scroll down to see additional options (Effect Size Methods and Significance Level)
        • Make desired changes to effective size and significance level selections (optional)
        • Click the Run Analysis button

      • Help Notes:
        • Customized properties for power analysis are available in the MPS-Db via a sidebar on the power analysis page
          • If the Run Analysis button is disabled, read the page prompts to see if a required selection has been omitted

    • Assay Data Reproducibility (Intra-Study)

      • To perform intra-study reproducibility analysis:
        • Go to the Studies tab/icon
        • Click on the View/Edit button next to the study of interest
        • Click the Analyze/Edit button and select the Reproducibility option
        • Use the Custom Filtering for Data Visualization (sidebar) to change grouping or filtering of data
        • Check the box next to the set of interest to see the results of interest
        • Scroll down page to detailed results section of interest (including chips, studies and each study's reproducibility status, Max CV, ICC, and graphs

      Assay Data Reproducibility (Inter-Study)

      • To perform inter-study reproducibility analysis:
        • Go to the Data Analysis tab/icon
        • Select the Graphing/Reproducibility option
        • Check at least one box in each list (work left to right, top to bottom)
          • Notice how checking and unchecking boxes changes the number of points (assay data points) that are selected for inclusion
        • Click on the Show Reproducibility button
        • Use the Custom Filtering for Data Visualization (sidebar) to change grouping or filtering of data
        • Check the box next to the set of interest to see the results of interest
        • Scroll down page to detailed results section of interest (including chips, studies and each study's reproducibility status, Max CV, ICC, and graphs
        • Explore the Reproducibility Properties options in the sidebar (optional)
        • Customized properties for reproducibility analysis are available in the MPS-Db via a sidebar on the inter-study reproducibility analysis page. To perform reproducibility analysis:
          • Reproducibility Comparison within Selected Data Sets - By Center (Center to Center) or By Study (Study to Study)
          • Normalize by Median - helpful if trends are similar but values are on different scales
          • Maximum Interpolation Size - maximum number of data points that can be interpolated, between experimental measurements, to allow for comparison within selected data sets, center to center or study to study (shown as stars on the reproducibility plots)

      • Help Notes:
        • By default, the screen will automatically refresh, to refresh manually, check the Manually Refresh box, make the desired changes, then click on the Refresh button to apply changes

    • Assay Data Study Sets

      • To add a study set:
        • Go to the Studies tab/icon
        • Select the Add Study Set option
        • Select the studies to include in the set by putting a check mark in the box
        • Click the Continue button
        • Fill out the form
        • Click the Select Assays button to limit data to include in the study set (optional)
        • Click the Submit button to save

      • Help Notes:
        • A study set is conceptually similar to an inter-study analysis, with the added functionality to:
          • Save the selection of the study set for later retrieval
          • Assign the study set a URL for easy sharing (e.g., inclusion in a publication)
          • Data from each study in the study set can be limited to a subset of one or more assays
        • Viewers of a study set created by another user will only see data from studies to which they have access (i.e. a study set does NOT override existing permissions to studies)

    • Assay Data Collaborators

        • A collaborator group is a means of allowing data from a private study (a study that has not been signed off) to be shared with another user who is not a member of the same data group (e.g., someone from another lab)
        • Using a collaborator group is currently the only way to allow someone from another data team to see data contained in a study prior to either signing off a study (after sign off, the Assay Data Accessors feature can be used) or opening the study to the public
        • Coming soon - data providers will be able to share data with selected colleagues without assistance from MPS-Db administrators
        • Currently, data are shared using collaborator groups that are managed by MPS-Db administrators

    • Assay Data Accessors

      • About Accessor Groups:
        • An accessor group is a means of allowing data from a study during the year embargo period (after sign off and before public release) to be shared with selected colleagues who are not members of the same data group (e.g., someone from another lab)
        • Currently, access groups must be created and edited by an MPS-Db administrator

    • PBPK Analysis

      • To perform PBPK analysis:
        • Go to the Data Analysis tab/icon
        • Select the PBPK option
        • Check at least one box in each list (work left to right, top to bottom)
          • Notice how checking and unchecking boxes changes the number of assay data points selected for inclusion
        • Click the Show PBPK button
        • Use the Custom Filtering for Data Visualization (sidebar) to change filtering of data (optional)
        • Check the box next to the set of interest to see the results of interest
        • The analyses that can be performed are:
          • Calculate the clearance of a compound
          • Predict the plasma concentration given a dose and dose interval
          • Predict the dose and dose interval to reach a specific plasma level

      • Help Notes:
        • A significant amount of the information used in the PBPK analysis is manually curated into the database
        • Currently, the required parameters and rates have been added to the database for the liver
        • Parameters and rates in yellow can be changed while performing an analysis
        • Depending on the analysis being performed, some yellow boxes can remain empty

    • Disease Portal (Clinical Trials and Study Data Combined via a Disease)

      • To access the disease portal:
        • Go to the Models tab/icon
        • Select the View Diseases option
        • Click on the View button next to the disease of interest
        • Review the table that summarizes disease related information available in the MPS-Db
        • Click on one of the options for more information about the disease
          • Disease Overview - disease basics
          • Disease Biology - active links to relevant biology/genomic information
          • Clinical Data - summarized findings from clinical trials.gov
          • Disease Models & Studies - list of disease related models and studies related to the disease that are in the MPS-Db

      • Help Notes:
        • The study list on the Disease Models & Studies page and the summary counts on the main Diseases page are permission specific
        • For more information on the reference data source(s), click here

    • Compound Report (Chemical Properties and Study Data Combined via a Compound)

      • To access a compound report:
        • Go to the Compounds tab/icon
        • Select the Compound Report option
        • As a precaution, click the Clear Current Selections button
        • Use the search box to limit the compound list (optional)
        • Check the boxes for the compounds to include
        • If desired, use the search bar to get a different compound list
        • If desired, check additional compounds to include
        • Click Generate Compound Report button
        • If a blue plus sign is present, click it to see aggregated assay study data

      • Help Notes:
        • Compound selections are cumulative
        • To start with a fresh search, click the Clear Current Selections button
        • To save compound selections, click the Save Selections button (when returning at a later time, click Load Selections button)
        • Only studies that have been checked for inclusion in the compound report will be included in the aggregation
        • For more information on the reference data source(s), click here

      • To add a reference to the MPS-Db:
        • Go to the Studies tab/icon

        • Fill out the form
        • Click the Submit button to save

    Assay Data Customization Options

    Graph customization is not saved, it lasts only until the user navigates away from the page.

      • Customized grouping and filtering is available in the MPS-Db via a sidebar where data are available for review. To customize:
        • If hidden, click the Toggle Sidebar button to show the sidebar
        • Click on the parameter menus (Study Parameters, Cell Parameters, Compound Parameters, Setting Parameters) to see options
        • To filter - click on the filter symbol
          • To select data, check the box next to the data
          • To omit data, uncheck the box next to the data
        • To group - check/uncheck the box in the Group column
          • Check the box to include parameter in grouping
          • Uncheck the box to remove parameter from grouping
        • Repeat for all attributes to override

      • Help Notes:
        • In general, increasing the number of parameters selected for grouping will increase the number of data groups in the summary data aggregation table
        • In general, increasing the number of filters will decrease the number of data groups in the summary data aggregation tables (or decrease the size of the group)
        • By default, the screen will automatically refresh, to refresh manually, check the Manually Refresh box, make the desired changes, then click on the Refresh button to apply changes
      • General graph customization is available via a sidebar where graphs are displayed. To customize:
        • If hidden, click the Toggle Sidebar button to show the sidebar
        • Click on the property menus to see options
        • To change the general graph properties, click on the menu of the property of interest in the General Graph Properties section
          • Plot Graph By - select By Chips (chips and wells), By Group (groups as defined based on choices made in the Grouping/Filtering section), or By Compound to indicate how to aggregate assay data
          • Data Aggregation - select Arithmetic Mean, Geometric Mean, or Median (additional options available based on selection)
          • Tooltip Display - select Each Data Point or Entire Time Point (when hovering over a point on a graph)
          • Data Display - option to select Include Excluded Data and/or treat Negative Values as 0
          • Time Unit - select Day, Hour, or Minute for time unit of x-axis

      • Help Notes:
        • By default, the screen will automatically refresh, to refresh manually, check the Manually Refresh box, make the desired changes, then click on the Refresh button to apply changes
      • Individualized graphing options are available via a graph popup where graphs are displayed. To customize:
        • Right-click on an individual graph (on touch screen - press and hold one finger while tapping with another)
        • Select the desired individualized graph options
          • Show Plot - if uncheck and apply, graph will not be displayed on main page
          • Percent Control - when box is checked, assays and time points without associated controls are removed and the y-axis units are changed to percent control
          • Dose-Response - changes x-axis from time to dose
          • X-axis to Log Scale - transforms x-axis
          • Y-axis to Log Scale - transforms y-axis
        • Click the View Preview button
        • Make changes to the general graph customization features as desired (optional)
        • Click the Apply to Plot button to apply changes, the Revert to Default button to remove graph specific customization, or the Cancel to go back to the last applied version

    • Bioactivities

      • To view bioactivities:
        • Go to the Compounds tab/icon
        • Select the View Bioactivities option

      • Option 1 - Quick Search

      • To perform quick search:
        • Enter at least one search string in one of the fields (Compound, Target, and/or Name)
          • Notice that options appear as you type in the boxes, these can be selected from the dropdown
        • Click on the Exclude ... buttons to change data selection as indicated on the button (optional)
        • Click on the button to perform the search

      • Bioactivities Quick Search Example

        • Follow these steps:
          • Enter Compound = "ASPIRIN"
          • Enter Target = "Blood"
          • Enter Name = "IC50"
          • Click on the

      • Option 2 - Advanced Search

      • To perform an advanced search:
        • Click on the Exclude ... button or the Use PubChem... button to change data selection as indicated on the button (optional)
        • Enter a search string in one or more of the filters (Bioactivities, Targets, Compounds) (optional)
        • Check all the boxes needed to indicate desired selections
        • Click the Generate Table button

      • Bioactivities Advanced Search Example

        • Follow these steps:
          • Select Target Types - check the box in the top left to include all
          • Select Organisms - check the box in the top left to include all
          • Filter Compounds - check all boxes, leave LogP and Molecular Weight blank
          • Bioactivities Filter - type in "IC50"
          • Select Bioactivities - check the box in the top left to include all
          • Targets Filter - type in "Blood"
          • Select Targets - check the box in the top left to include all
          • Compounds Filter - type in "ASPIRIN"
          • Select Compounds - check the box in the top left to include all
          • Click the button to Generate Table
      • Help Notes:
        • The Data Validity column of the results table/list indicates whether or not, and why, an entry was marked "questionable"
        • Not every ChEMBL entry has a corresponding PubChem entry, and vice versa
        • The following will clear all selections
          • Changing between ChEMBL and PubChem; Changing whether questionable results are excluded or not; Changing the min feature count; Changing the Target Types or Organisms
        • The Minimum Feature Count Per Record field specifies the number of bioactivities that need to be linked to a given option for it to appear
          • e.g. if the min feature count is 10, then the compound "ALDRIN" will not be an option because only 5 bioactivities link to "ALDRIN"
          • Click the Apply button to confirm the change to the min feature count
        • For more information on the reference data source(s), click here

    • Drug Trials

      • To view drug trials data:
        • Go to the Compounds tab/icon
        • Select the View Drug Trials option
        • Use the search box to limit the list (optional)
        • Click on the View button next to the drug trial of interest to see compound details
        • Click on the Previous button or the Next button to scroll through the drug trials (optional)

      • Help Notes:
        • For more information on the reference data source(s), click here

    • Adverse Events

      • To view adverse events:
        • Go to the Compounds tab/icon
        • Select the View Adverse Events option
        • Use the search box to limit the list (optional)
          • Use a space in the search to include multiple terms (e.g, "EPINEPHRINE nausea")
        • Click on the View button to see details of the compound and event of interest
        • Click on the Toggle Plot button next to an event of interest to generate time plots of reported events
          • Scroll down to the bottom of the page to see the plot
          • Use the data boxes under the plot to control time frame of plot (optional)
        • Click on the Previous button or Next button to scroll through the compounds (optional)

      • Help Notes:
        • Only top adverse events for compounds are shown in the results of the search
        • The Number of Reports column indicates the number of reports that list both the compound and adverse event in the row
        • The number of reports does not contain any information regarding the dose of the drug
        • A compound can be listed in a report even if it is not suspected of causing the adverse event
        • For more information on the reference data source(s), click here

    • Compare Adverse Events

      • To compare adverse events:
        • Go to the Compounds tab/icon
        • Select the Compare Adverse Events option
        • As a precaution, click the Clear Current Selections button
        • Use the search box to limit the list (optional)
        • Check the boxes of the events to include
        • Check the boxes of the compounds to include
          • At least one compound and one event must be selected before data will show on the graph
        • If desired, use the search bar to get different lists
        • If desired, check additional compounds and/or events to include
        • Scroll down to see the graph comparing events
        • If desired, click on the Show Raw Counts/Hide Raw Counts button to toggle from Number of Reports and Number of Reports per 10,000 Mentions

      • Help Notes:

    • Heatmap of Bioactivities

      • To generate a heatmap:
        • Go to the Data Analysis tab/icon
        • Select the Heatmap option
        • Click on the Exclude ... button or the Use PubChem... button to change data selection as indicated on the button (optional)
        • Enter a search string in one or more of the filters (Bioactivities, Targets, Compounds, Drug Trials) (optional)
        • Check all the boxes needed to indicate desired selections
        • The Minimum Feature Count Per Record field specifies the number of bioactivities that need to be linked to a given option for it to appear

        • If desired, click on a row or column label to sort the heatmap such that entries in the selection are ordered from highest to lowest
        • If desired, click the Download the Heatmap button (downloads data used in heatmap to csv)
        • Scroll to the bottom of the heatmap to see the legend of colors used
        • Clicking on a specific square in the heatmap will show that particular value in a pop-up window

    • Make Treatment Groups - Groups Tab

      • Add the required information as follows:
        • Select an MPS Model
        • Select an MPS Model Version (information about cells and settings will be pulled from the model version, if available)
        • Apply to the Group
        • The Type is set based on the device used in the MPS Model (plate or chip device)
        • Enter the treatment Group Name
        • Enter the Number of Chips in the group
        • Change the Test Type for each group, as needed
        • Add compounds, additional cells, and additional settings by clicking on the Add Compound, Add Cell, or Add Setting button, respectively
        • Click on the Add Group button or click the Copy button (in a Group) to make a new Group
        • Review differences between groups in the Group Difference Display table
        • Click the Submit button to save or the Next button to continue

      • Help Notes:
        • There are many options for the order of adding Groups (e.g., a Group can be copied at any time as many times as needed)
        • If an MPS Model Version has not been made and/or has not been populated correctly, cells and settings will not be automatically pulled into the group setup
        • An MPS Model Version is a starting point for a treatment group (if changes are made to cells, settings, and/or compounds, a model version will no longer fully describe the condition of the chip)
        • Treatment compounds will need to be added as they are not part of the MPS model version (compounds cannot be saved as part of a model version)
        • Chips are numbered sequentially, starting from 1 (these can be changed, if necessary)
        • Remove cells, settings, and/or compounds by clicking the red X's (click on individual X's or, to remove a column, click on the red X in the column header, and to remove a row, click on the red X in the row label)
        • Enter a treatment group for each treatment condition (for wells in a plate and for chips)
        • Treatment groups will be applied to plates (if a plate device was selected) in subsequent steps

      There are multiple ways of uploading data into the MPS-Db, including:

      3) uploading omic formatted data in specific omic data format(s).


      Processed MIF-C-a Format

      • To add processed data in MIF-C-a format:
        • Prepare a data file (MIF-C-a)
        • Make sure that the Target, Method, Unit, and Chip and Well Ids all match, exactly, what was entered as part of the study setup
        • In the Upload Data tab, click the button to add processed data files
        • To get an example template file, click here

      • Processed Data Help Notes:
        • The ONLY assay data that are editable in the MPS-GUI is the Exclude field (editable on the study item edit page)
        • Changes made to the Exclude field using the MPS-GUI will be lost when data are re-uploaded
        • To replace assay data values with new values, remove the data file from the Current Data Files, then correct the values in the MIF-C-a file and re-upload that file
        • Chip and well Ids must be unique within a study
        • Each file (and each workbook if using Excel) should contain data for only one study
        • Data for one study may be submitted in multiple files (and/or multiple tabs of an Excel workbook)
        • If uploading an Excel workbook, workbook can contain multiple tabs
        • All files (or tabs) with correct headers (i.e. column names) will be evaluated for upload, others will be ignored
        • Correct headers must be placed in the first three rows to be considered valid

    • Validation, Flags and Notes (optional)

      To facilitate data review and validation, the database offers the option to flag and add notes to a 1) study, 2) study item (i.e. a chip or a well), and 3) plate. When a study is flagged, a flag is display in the Review column for that study in the study list (a note is displayed as a hover tip). When a study item is flagged, the flag is displayed in the Review column for that study item in the study item list. In addition, Study Items can be 'validated' (a check mark will show in the Review column for the Study Item in the Study Item list).


      Study Flags and Notes

      • To add flags and or notes to a study:
        • Go to the study of interest
        • Click the Analyze/Edit button
        • Select the Edit This Study option
        • Click on the at the bottom of the page (the Details Tab)
        • Click on the Notes button to add a note (adding a note is optional)
        • Click Submit to save

      Study Item Flags and Notes

      • To add flags and or notes to a study item:
        • Go to the study of interest
        • Scroll down to the Chips and Wells section of the page
        • Click the View/Edit button for the study item (chip or well) of interest
        • Click on the at the bottom of the page
        • Click on the Notes button to add a note (adding a note is optional)
        • Click Submit to save

      Study Item Validation

      • To add a validation check mark to a study item:
        • Go to the study of interest
        • Scroll down to the Chips and Wells section of the page
        • Click the View/Edit button for the study item (chip or well) of interest
        • Click on the Click Here to Mark this Entry as Validated button near the top of the page
        • Click Submit to save

      • Help Notes:
        • Validation check marks, flags, and notes are visible to all users who have permission to view a study
        • Validation, flags, and notes can be used during the validation process, and removed prior to sharing a study (i.e. study sign-off)
        • Study item validation is designed to be part of the data provider's QC process (it is optional and is not required to be completed prior to study sign off)

    • Data Sharing - Sign-Off a Study (optional)

      Data group administrators can sign-off a study in their data group.
